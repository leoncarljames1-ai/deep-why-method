How to Use The Evidence

Your weekly check-in, journal and standards record. Everything in one place. Everything saved to your device.

The Evidence is where you track who you are becoming. Not what you are escaping. Every check-in, every reflection, every moment you hold your standard becomes part of a record that shows you, in numbers and in your own words, that the work is working.

You set it up once. You come back every week. Over time your chart climbs and your record grows into proof you can hold.

Step one

Setting up your foundation

The first time you open The Evidence you will be asked to fill in your foundation. This happens once and everything you enter here stays with you throughout the programme. Take your time with it.

1
Your Big Why
Not a goal. The reason you are doing this at all. The thing that makes stopping not an option. Write it honestly.
2
Your Big Vision
Who are you when this work is done? What does your life look like? Write the picture in detail.
3
The identity you are becoming
Complete the sentence: I am... One line. Make it feel true, not aspirational.
4
Your non-negotiables
Three commitments you are making. These do not bend regardless of how you feel on any given day.
5
Your intentions
What are you here to shift? Be specific about what you are carrying into this programme.
6
Your This
The specific thing or things you are here to overcome. You can add up to three. These feed into The Standard feature.
Once it is set

Your Big Why appears at the top of your dashboard every time you return. Your foundation is always there when you need reminding of why you started.

The four check-ins

How the week works

The Evidence has four ways to check in. Each serves a different moment in the week. Use them in order for the full experience.

Start of Week
Use this at the beginning of each week. Set your intentions, name three commitments, and plan your act of self-worth and act of service for the week ahead.
Daily Check-in
A quick check-in you can do any time, any day. Rate your mood as a percentage and write whatever needs to come out. No structure. No prompts. Just space. Do this at least three times a week.
End of Week
Done at the end of the week. Rate yourself across five dimensions, review your commitments, record your acts of self-worth and service, note your gratitude and what shifted. This is what feeds your chart.
The Standard
Use this any time a decision is in front of you. Your identity and non-negotiables are shown to you. Five questions slow you down and make you look honestly at the decision. Then you name it and log it. The moment becomes part of your record.

The chart

Your progress in numbers

After your first End of Week check-in your chart appears. The gold line is your weekly average score across all five dimensions. The small cream dots are your daily mood check-ins plotted through the week.

The chart uses a zoomed-in view of your actual score range rather than showing a fixed scale from zero. This means every movement shows clearly. A shift from 56% to 74% over eight weeks fills the chart and looks like what it is: real progress.

A dashed trend line shows the overall direction of travel across all the noise and dips. When a week dips, the trend line shows the direction everything is still heading.

The five dimensions

What you are rating

1
Alignment
How aligned do you feel with who you are becoming? Are your actions matching the identity you set at the start?
2
Clarity
How clear do you feel about your direction? Clarity is one of the first things that shifts when the work is working.
3
Mood and state
How has your overall state been this week? Not just one day but the general tone of the week.
4
Productivity
How productive have you been? Are you moving things forward or standing still?
5
Momentum
Are you moving forward? This is the feeling of building, not just doing.

The Standard

In the moment of decision

The Standard is not a weekly check-in. It is a tool for the exact moment a decision is sitting in front of you and you are not sure which way to go.

Open it any time. It shows you your identity and your non-negotiables before you answer anything. Then five questions slow you down. The last step asks you to name what you are facing in your own words and place it inside the OTOA sentence.

The sentence

If I can overcome [what you write] I can overcome anything.

After you log the moment, that sentence is all you see. Nothing else. You sit with it.

If you toggle Check back on this, The Evidence will surface that entry the next time you open it and ask how it went. That follow-up updates your Standards held count on your dashboard.

Your PDF and restore code

Saving your record

Your data saves automatically to this device every time you check in. As long as you use the same browser on the same device your record is always there when you return.

Switching devices or clearing your browser?
Download your PDF from the PDF button in the top bar. At the bottom of the PDF is a restore code.

On the new device, open The Evidence. Tap Returning? Restore a previous record on the setup screen. Paste the restore code and your full record comes back.

You can also tap Copy restore code inside the Your Foundation section on your dashboard at any time. This copies the code to your clipboard without needing to download the PDF.

Common questions

Quick answers

How often should I use The Evidence?
Start of Week once per week. Daily check-in at least three times per week. End of Week once per week to close it out. The Standard whenever a decision needs it.
What is The This tracker at the end of End of Week?
A small personal gauge at the bottom of your End of Week check-in. It is not on your main chart. Rate how loud The This is speaking this week. 1 means quiet and in control. 10 means it is overcoming you. Watching this number fall over weeks is its own kind of proof.
Can I edit my foundation after I set it?
Yes. Tap Setup in the top bar of your dashboard at any time to update your Big Why, Vision, Identity, Non-Negotiables, Intentions or Your This. Your weekly records are kept when you edit setup.
What do the milestones mean?
At weeks 1, 4, 8 and 12 a message appears to mark the moment. There is also a milestone for the first time your weekly score reaches 70% or above. These are not badges. They are just moments worth acknowledging.
What if I miss a week?
Come back when you can. The record does not judge a gap. Start the next week and keep going. Consistency matters more than perfection.
What does Standards held mean on the dashboard?
When you log a Standard moment and toggle Check back on this, The Evidence asks you later how it went. If you chose I held it, that counts as a standard held. The ratio shows you how often you are backing your own decisions with action.
