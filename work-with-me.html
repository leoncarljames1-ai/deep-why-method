When 1:1 work makes sense

Self-guided work is powerful. But some things move faster and more completely with someone else tracking what you cannot track in yourself.

These are the signals:

  • The same pattern keeps returning despite multiple sessions with the method.
  • The why chain consistently reaches a wall and stops without producing a clear root.
  • Something surfaces during the process that feels too heavy to sit with alone.
  • You want to move faster than self-guided work allows.
  • You know what the problem is but cannot seem to shift it on your own.

In a 1:1 session the method works differently. Someone else can track your body language, your pauses, the moment your voice changes. They can ask the right question at the right time. They can stay with you in the material in a way a written process cannot.

I have been coaching men through this work for six years. I trained three years as a counsellor. I developed the Eye to Eye Method — a process that finds the belief behind a behaviour by working through three layers: what the behaviour is giving you on the surface, what you are actually craving underneath, and the original wound that was never processed.

Every session uses both the Deep Why Method and the Eye to Eye Method together.
