The Deep Why Method

A guided process. Type your answers as you go. Take your time with each step.

Choose where you want to start. Both routes lead to the same place.

Start with a belief
You have a sense of something you hold about yourself that limits you. Use the cognition list to find it.
Start with a problem
You have a specific behaviour, event, or situation that is still sitting with you. Start from that.

Close your eyes before each question and feel the answer before you type. If you stay in your head, you will get thinking. This works on what you feel.

Step 3
The Exit Question
Feel your way into the answer. Do not think your way into it.
Your belief

"Who am I without this belief?"

Close your eyes. Hold the question. Feel what comes before you write it. If the last answer in your why chain was itself a belief, hold that one instead. Whichever has the most weight is the right one.

Now bring all of that together. Feel it spreading through you in every direction — in front and behind, left and right, above and below.

Does the belief still have weight?

Not fully positive? Ask yourself: what would it feel like to let this go? Or: what do I need to feel right now? Feel it, then try the question again.

Future Identity
Phase A — Enter the Scene
You are stepping into who you are without it
Phase A
The opposite of what you started with. It is shown below — read it, feel it, then continue.

A  Think of a future or goal where this is fully alive in you. Close your eyes and step into that scene. See where you are. Hear what is around you. Feel yourself already there.

B  Now look at the future version of you from the outside. His posture. His presence. His expression. Then step inside him. You are him now.

"I am [your positive cognition]."

Say it out loud. See it as true. Feel it as known, not hoped for. Where do you feel it in your body?

If anything negative interrupts: ask "what do I need to feel right now?" Feel it. Reinforce it. Return to the scene.

Phase C — Ground and Rehearse
Every action you take from here deepens the conviction
Phase C
Be specific. A real action, a real time.

A  Return to the scene.

Read the belief above and your first action. Close your eyes. Step back into the scene. See yourself living from this. This is who you are.

B  Test it.

Bring in a real obstacle. Something that could actually get in the way. See it clearly. Feel the weight of it — the resistance, the doubt, the moment you might want to stop. Now see yourself choosing to move through it anyway. Watch yourself come out the other side. Run it twice. Return to your future scene. End on success.
