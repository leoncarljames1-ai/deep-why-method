Before You Begin

Read this once before your first session. It tells you what this method is, what it is not, and what to do if it becomes overwhelming.

What this is

The Deep Why Method is a self-development tool. It works by following a feeling or belief down to its root through a chain of why questions. When you find what is at the bottom and clear it, the belief that was built on top of it loses its reason to exist.

It is not therapy. It is not counselling. It is a structured process you run on yourself. Most people find it direct and practical. Some find it goes deeper than they expected.

What it is not

It is not a substitute for professional mental health support. If you are currently in crisis, experiencing severe depression, PTSD, or a diagnosed psychiatric condition, speak to a qualified professional before working with this material.

It is also not something you need to force. If something does not feel right at any point, stop. Use the Reset Breath on the next screen to settle yourself, then decide whether to continue or close the session.

Important

This method works at depth. Emotions, memories, and physical sensations may surface during the process. This is normal. It is part of what makes it work.

If it becomes overwhelming at any point, stop immediately and use the Reset Breath. Both continuing and stopping are the right call at different times. Know which one this is.

By using this method you are taking full responsibility for your own wellbeing and approaching this work as a self-directed individual, not as a patient under clinical care. Leon James accepts no liability for the outcomes of your use of this material.

The Reset Breath
Use this any time the process becomes overwhelming

If at any point during a session the material feels like too much, stop and use this technique to return to ground. Learn it before you need it.

Sit upright. Both feet on the floor. Place one hand on your chest and one on your stomach. You are going to breathe in three distinct phases. Do not rush any of them.

The Reset Breath — Three Cycles
1
Inhale — 5 counts
Through the nose. Breathe into the stomach first, then the chest. Fill completely.
2
Hold — 2 counts
Stay still. Feel the pause.
3
Exhale — 7 counts
Through the mouth. Slow and controlled. Release everything. On each exhale, consciously release the tension in your jaw, your shoulders, and your hands.

Repeat for three full cycles. Let your body do the work.

Once your breathing has settled and you feel grounded, decide: return to the process if you are ready, or close the session if the material requires it.

If you are in the UK, the Samaritans are available 24 hours a day on 116 123. In a mental health crisis, contact your GP or go to your nearest A&E.

How This Works
What to expect from each part of the course

The course is built in four parts. Each one has its own lesson. Here is what each part does and how they connect.

Part 1 — Find What Needs Clearing

You identify what you are working on. Either a belief you hold about yourself from the cognition list, or a specific problem, event, or behaviour that is still sitting with you. Both routes are in the next lesson.

Part 2 — The Deep Why Method

This is the interactive tool. You follow the belief or problem down through a chain of why questions until you reach the root. The tool guides you through every step and generates a session summary at the end that you can copy and keep.

You choose your route at the start — belief or problem. Both lead to the same place.

Part 3 — Future Identity

Once the clearing is done, you step into who you are without it. This is a three-phase visualisation process. Phase A enters the scene. Phase B builds conviction. Phase C grounds it in action and rehearses it against a real obstacle.

Future Identity is built into the main tool and also available as a standalone lesson you can return to on its own, without running a full why chain session first.

One thing to know before you start

Close your eyes before each question and feel the answer before you type. If you stay in your head, you will get thinking. This method works on what you feel, not what you reason through.

Take your time with each step. There is no rush.

If you are doing this between sessions with Leon, bring your session summary to the next call. It gives him context on what came up and where the work went.
