The Deep Why Method

Find What Needs Clearing

Two routes into the method. Read both, then choose the one that fits what you are bringing today.

Every session starts with one question: what are you working on? The answer shapes which route you take into the tool.

Route 1

Starting from a belief

You use this route when you have a sense that you hold something about yourself that is limiting you — but you cannot always name it precisely. That is what the cognition list is for.

Go to the cognition list in the tool and read slowly. Do not rush through it looking for the most dramatic one. Read each line and let your body respond. You are looking for recognition — a tightening, a feeling, something that says that one. That is your starting point.

How the why chain works

Once you have your cognition, you ask why it is true. Feel the answer before you type it. Then ask why again. Keep going. The chain will move in one of two directions.

It can go inward — "because I'm worthless, because I never get anything right, because I was told I wasn't enough." That is exactly where this work lives. Follow it.

Or it can go outward — "because he's jealous, because she never listened." The moment the answer becomes about what someone else is, thinks, or feels, stop. That is not your root. Move to the exit question.

Example — how a chain might move
1
Why is this true?
Because I always mess things up
2
Why?
Because I panic when things matter
3
Why?
Because deep down I don't believe I deserve it to work out

Stop when you hit something raw that needs no further question. That is the root.

When you ask "who am I without this belief?" it refers back to the original cognition you started with. But if the last answer in your why chain is itself a belief — something like "because I am not enough" — hold that one instead. Whichever has the most weight in that moment is the right one.

You can also write your own belief in the tool if nothing on the list fits. There is a space at the bottom of the cognition list for this.

Route 2

Starting from a problem

You use this route when you have a specific behaviour, event, or situation that is still sitting with you. Something you keep going back to, something you cannot seem to move past, something that happened that still has weight.

Name it in a few words. Give it an intensity score so you have a baseline. Then hold the image of the worst part of it — the moment it felt most intense, the way someone looked, what was said, the moment it happened. Hold it fully before you start asking why.

The same rules apply

Ask why it affects you. Feel each answer before you type it. Stop when the answer goes raw, or the moment it turns into a story about someone else's actions or motivations.

The exit question on this route is: who am I without this problem. If the last answer in your why chain is a belief, hold that when you ask the question. Whichever has the most weight in that moment is the right one.

The optional question "Why can't I let this go?" sits above the why chain in the tool. Sit with it before you start the numbered whys. Only answer it if something comes. Do not force it.

Both routes lead to the same place. The route is just how you get in. Once you are in the why chain the process is identical.
