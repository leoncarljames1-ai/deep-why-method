The Eye to Eye Method

A process for finding what is underneath. Not the behaviour. What the behaviour is trying to give you.

This is not an exercise in thinking. It is an exercise in observing. When you read a question, close your eyes. Allow whatever comes up to come up. Do not analyse it. Do not look for the right answer. Whatever image, thought, sensation or feeling appears — that is what you work with.

This work may activate strong emotional material. If at any point it feels overwhelming, stop. Use the Reset Breath from the Before You Begin lesson and seek support if needed.

Choose your route.

Addictive Behaviour
A compulsive pattern, sexual behaviour, fantasy or fetish you want to understand and resolve.
Problem or Event
A specific situation, memory or emotional pattern that is still sitting with you.
Addictive Behaviour — Step 1
Identify the Behaviour
Be specific. Clarity strengthens the process.
Step 1

We are not working with explicit content. We are working with the emotional charge, the psychological meaning, and the unmet need beneath the behaviour. This requires honesty and emotional willingness.

Time of day, situation, emotional state that precedes it.
How strongly does this pattern have a hold on you right now? Tap a number.
Addictive Behaviour — Step 2
The Peak Moment
Identify what it is giving you
Step 2

Picture yourself fully in the behaviour or experience. Find the single moment that feels the most intense or satisfying. Hold that image in full, vivid detail before you answer.

Describe it. The more specific, the more this works.
Focus on the positive or pleasurable feelings. Shame may interfere — stay with it gently.
Examples: I am powerful. I am wanted. I am in control. I am enough.

Now amplify all of these feelings as much as you can. Stay with them. Let them build.

Addictive Behaviour — Step 3
Step Outside Yourself
Observe from the outside
Step 3

Imagine taking a step outside yourself. Turn around and look directly into your own eyes. Stay in third person. Observe — do not analyse.

As you observe, look for these three layers:

Layer 1
The Surface
The pleasure, intensity, power or relief. What the behaviour gives on the surface.
Layer 2
The Craving
The longing beneath. To feel loved, safe, accepted, chosen, significant, valued.
Layer 3
The Unmet Need
Often painful. Emptiness, loneliness, rejection, feeling unseen, not knowing who you are.
Look for the craving and the unmet need. Stay with it. Lean into it.

When you feel deeply connected to this innermost layer — continue.

Float back to an earlier memory.

Allow a memory to arise naturally. Do not force it. What comes up?

Go and give your younger self what they need right now. In your mind, step toward them. Say what needs to be said. Do what needs to be done. Amplify this as much as possible.

Addictive Behaviour — Step 4
Return to the Behaviour
Check what has shifted
Step 4

Go back to the original behaviour and peak moment you started with. Hold that image. Notice what has changed.

Score it again now. If it is not at zero, repeat the process until it gets there.

If the score is not at zero, go back to Step 3 and repeat. The process is designed to be run more than once. Each pass goes deeper.

Addictive Behaviour — Final Step
Future Self Connection
Step into who you are becoming
Integration

Meet the version of you who no longer holds that old belief or pattern. See them clearly.

The version of you who is free of this. How do they move through the world?
Problem — Step 1
Identify the Problem
The more specific, the more the process works.
Step 1

Picture yourself in the single moment, recent event, behaviour or problem that feels the most emotionally intense. Hold that image in full, vivid detail. The more specific, the more this works.

How intense does this feel right now?
The way someone looked, what was said, the moment it happened. The most disturbing image.
What do you believe about yourself when you hold this?
Problem — Step 2
Step Outside Yourself
Observe from the outside
Step 2

Hold the image and the negative thought together. Feel the physical sensations in your body. Now imagine going outside yourself into third person. Look deeply and directly into your own eyes.

Float back to childhood.

Let your mind scan back to the earliest memory where you felt this same way. Allow it to come naturally. Do not force it.

Go and give your younger self what they need right now. Say what needs to be said. Do what needs to be done. Let the feeling express itself fully toward them.

Problem — Step 3
Return to the Problem
Check what has shifted
Step 3

Go back to the original situation or event you started with. Hold that image again. Notice what has changed.

If it is not at zero, repeat the process until it gets there.

If the score is not at zero, go back and repeat. Each pass goes deeper. Trust the process.

Problem — Final Step
Future Self Connection
Step into who you are becoming
Integration

Meet the version of you who no longer holds that old belief or thought pattern. Place them in a scenario similar to the original event.

Session complete.

Take one slow breath. Notice how you feel in your body right now.
