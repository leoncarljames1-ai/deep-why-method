The OTOA Programme

How to Use This Method

Read this before your first session. It will change how you approach everything that follows.

One session can shift something significant

Some things move completely in a single session. Others surface another layer underneath. That is not failure. That is the process working exactly as it should.

When you notice a behaviour returning, or a new belief making itself known, come back and run the method again. Use whichever starting point fits — cognition or problem — and follow it down.

There is no fixed number of times you should do this. Some people use it once and that is enough for the thing they worked on. Others come back to it regularly. The method does not wear out.

Things worth knowing before you start

The same cognition landing twice does not mean the first session did nothing. It may mean there is more underneath, or a different event attached to the same belief. Follow it down again. It will not go to the same place.

If the why chain produces a different root than it did the first time, work from that. Your body is pointing at what needs to move now, not what needed to move last time.

If you feel resistance to starting, that is information. Name it, give it an intensity score, and run the method on the resistance itself. Ask why you cannot begin. Follow it down. You will find something there.

Close your eyes before each question and feel the answer before you type. If you stay in your head, you will get thinking. This method works on what you feel, not what you reason through.

Your session summaries are worth keeping. Copy them at the end of each session and save them somewhere you can return to. Over time they become a record of where the work has gone and what has shifted.

When Things Feel Stuck
What to do when the process feels like it is not working

If the exit question does not shift

Sometimes you reach the exit question and the belief or problem still has weight. This does not mean the session has failed. It means there is more in the why chain.

Go back. Ask again. Look for the answer you skipped past, the one that felt too raw or too simple to write down. That one is usually the root.

If the answer points at someone else, stop there and move to the exit question. The root is never about what someone else did. It is about what that event made you believe about yourself.

If the why chain goes blank after a few answers

Close your eyes. Go back to the original belief or image. Feel it in your body. Ask why from the feeling, not from the last answer you wrote. The chain runs on sensation, not logic. If you are thinking your way through it, you have left the process.

If something surfaces that feels too heavy

Stop. Use the Reset Breath from the Before You Begin lesson to return to ground. You do not need to push through material that feels overwhelming. Close the session and return to it when you are ready.

If the same material keeps surfacing and feels consistently too heavy to work with alone, that is a signal that 1:1 support would serve you better than self-guided work. That is not a weakness. It is accurate self-knowledge.

The method is designed to be self-guided. But some things move faster and more completely with someone else in the room. There is no failure in knowing the difference.

Choosing Your Tool
Which tool to use and when

You have three tools in this programme

They are not a sequence you work through once and leave. They are tools you return to depending on what is present for you at any given time.

The Deep Why Method is the core tool. Use it when something is sitting with you — a belief, a behaviour, an event, a feeling you cannot name. It works on anything that has emotional weight.

The Beliefs Library is for deeper work on the beliefs that shape who you are as a man — across relationships, money, identity, sexuality, body image, and how you see the world. Use it when you want to go into a specific area of your life and work through what you are carrying there.

Future Identity can be used on its own, not just as the final step of a why chain session. Once a clearing has happened, returning to Future Identity reinforces the new belief and rehearses you as the man who already holds it. Use it after a session, or on its own when you want to deepen a belief that is already shifting.

You do not need to follow a set order. Use what fits what you are bringing today. The tools work together but they also work independently.

If you are not sure which tool to use, start with The Deep Why Method. Choose the cognition route if something is sitting in your chest but you cannot name it precisely. Choose the problem route if there is a specific situation, event, or behaviour that keeps coming up.
